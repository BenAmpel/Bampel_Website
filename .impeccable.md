## Design Context

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** Rigorous · Cutting-edge · Bold

**Voice:** Confident and precise — the voice of a researcher who publishes in top venues and doesn't apologize for it. Not boastful; the data does the talking. Technical without being obscure.

**Tone:** Direct. Minimal filler. Neon-lit terminal energy — like a well-organized research environment that happens to look like a hacker's workstation.

**Emotional goals:** Visitors should feel a combination of *impressed* and *oriented* — they immediately understand this is serious, high-output cybersecurity research, and they can navigate it effortlessly.

---

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme:** Dark-only. Cyberpunk neon on deep black. This is a deliberate identity signal in the security research space — it's not decorative whimsy, it's a coherent statement about the research domain. Light mode is a non-goal.

**Color palette:**
- Background: `#0a0a0a` (near-black, terminal)
- Primary accent: `#00ff41` (matrix green — CTAs, active states, progress)
- Secondary accent: `#00e5ff` / `#00ffea` (cyan — information, tabs, links)
- Tertiary accent: `#e040fb` / `#ff0055` (magenta — premium tiers, warnings, trend indicators)
- Text: `#c9d1d9` (cool light gray)
- Subtle surface: `#161b22` (dark navy, card backgrounds)
- Borders: `rgba(0,255,65,0.15–0.35)` (green-tinted glass edges)

**Typography:**
- Primary/code: `'Fira Code', 'Roboto Mono', monospace` — used for labels, headings, data
- Serif body: `'Merriweather'` — for long-form prose passages
- Display: `'Playfair Display'` — for featured/hero moments

**Motion & effects:**
- Glitch animations (`clip-path` + chromatic aberration in cyan/magenta) on headings
- Frosted glass cards: `backdrop-filter: blur(6–10px)` + semi-transparent dark backgrounds
- Grid overlay texture (`50px × 50px`) on section backgrounds
- Transitions: fast (150–250ms), purposeful — no decorative spinning

**Anti-references:** Generic academic CV sites (plain white, Times New Roman, blue links). Anything that reads as a WordPress faculty page. Overly playful or colorful portfolio sites that undermine scholarly gravitas.

---

### Design Principles

1. **Data is the hero.** Every visual element exists to surface research signal — publication quality, topic evolution, citation momentum. Decoration that doesn't carry information should be removed or subdued.

2. **Hierarchy over noise.** Dark backgrounds with neon accents create natural focus. Use accent colors sparingly: green for primary action/status, cyan for navigation/secondary, magenta only for contrast or premium signal.

3. **Earned boldness.** The cyberpunk aesthetic is intentional — it reflects the security research domain. Maintain it consistently rather than mixing in soft or corporate visual idioms. Be bold, but never gratuitous.

4. **Respect the reader's intelligence.** Visitors are researchers. Labels can be terse, interactions can be minimal, density is acceptable. Avoid over-explaining. Trust that hover states, tooltips, and progressive disclosure are enough.

5. **WCAG AA contrast on neon.** Neon colors on dark backgrounds are beautiful but can fail contrast ratios. All readable text must meet 4.5:1 against its background. Decorative glows and shadows do not substitute for legible text. Test especially: small labels, chart tick marks, and tooltip text.

### Technical Constraints

- **Framework:** Hugo + Wowchemy (minimal night), deployed on Netlify
- **Shortcode architecture:** Self-contained HTML+`<style>`+`<script>` per component; no shared component library
- **Dark/light detection:** `body.dark` (dark) vs `body:not(.dark)` (light) CSS selectors
- **No backend:** All data via static JSON in `/static/data/` fetched client-side
- **Font imports:** Google Fonts — Fira Code, Merriweather, Playfair Display
- **Charting:** ECharts (`/static/vendor/echarts/`), Leaflet for maps
- **Animations:** CSS keyframes only; no animation library dependency
